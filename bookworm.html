Bookworm

Bookdentity – a reading identity dashboard built from your Goodreads history.

Bookdentity is a concept web app that analyzes a reader’s Goodreads library and surfaces a “reading identity” — the patterns, moods, and micro-obsessions hidden in what they read.

The homepage presents a calm, editorial-style dashboard with:

  • A Reading Persona card summarizing your current vibe (e.g., “soft political romantic,” “cozy data witch,” “nostalgic realist”).
  • A genre constellation that shows which themes you orbit around most frequently over time.
  • A timeline of reading eras – fantasy phase, poetry phase, political theory phase – based on clustering your shelves and dates.

Under the hood, the concept uses exported Goodreads CSV data and groups books by genre, tone, and publication year. The UI highlights balance and discovery: which types of books you keep returning to, which voices are missing, and which titles shaped key seasons of your life.

I designed Bookdentity to feel like a mirror for readers – not just a tracker. It turns raw lists into a narrative about who you are when you read, and who you might become if you explore outside your usual comfort genres.
