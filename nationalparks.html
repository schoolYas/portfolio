National Parks Website Refresh

A modernized National Park Service mockup homepage concept focused on storytelling, trip planning, and accessibility.

This concept reimagines the National Park Service homepage as a story-first, visitor-centered experience. Instead of a dense, text-heavy layout, the hero features a full-bleed photograph with a seasonal tagline (here: “Winter is Coming”) and a single primary call-to-action: Plan Your Visit.

Below the hero, a split planner helps users quickly answer the two most common questions:

  • “Find a Park” – filter by state, activity, and accessibility.
  • “Explore America’s Story” – browse editorial content by topic, era, or theme.

The page continues with a grid of featured stories, current conditions, and seasonal highlights. Each card uses consistent typography, soft shadows, and a muted color palette inspired by park signage: deep forest green, sandstone, and off-white. The goal is to feel elevated yet approachable, like a coffee table book meets a trip planner.

On the UX side, the redesign emphasizes:

  • Clear hierarchy for new vs. returning visitors.
  • Prominent links to safety information and accessibility resources.
  • Mobile-first card layouts that collapse cleanly on smaller screens.
  • Readable, high-contrast text over photography.

This project reflects my love for national parks, thoughtful interface design, and building experiences that invite people into nature with warmth, clarity, and a sense of wonder.
